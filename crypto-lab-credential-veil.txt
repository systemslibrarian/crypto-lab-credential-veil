- Repo name:         crypto-lab-credential-veil
- Short name (H1):   Credential Veil
- Subtitle:          Anonymous credentials · BBS+ selective disclosure
- One-liner:         Prove you're over 18 from a signed credential containing your birth date, without showing the birth date, without showing the signature, and without the verifier being able to link this presentation to the last one.
- Concept to teach:  A signed credential is an all-or-nothing artifact: to prove one field, you hand over the whole document and the signature that binds it. BBS+ breaks that. The holder proves knowledge of a signature over N messages while revealing only the k they choose — and the presentation is randomized, so two showings of the same credential are unlinkable. The issuer signed once; the holder can prove forever, differently each time.
- Primitives/spec:   BBS+ signatures (Boneh-Boyen-Shacham lineage, Camenisch-Drijvers-Lehmann), BLS12-381 pairings, multi-message signing, selective disclosure via proof of knowledge, presentation randomization, predicate proofs (age ≥ 18 via range proof), revocation via accumulator or status list
- Accent (--accent): #7E22CE
- Favicon emoji:     🎭
- In scope:
    * Real BBS+ over BLS12-381 via @noble/curves (link pairing-gate for the pairing math — do not rebuild it). Real multi-message signing, real proof generation, real verification.
    * The all-or-nothing baseline: an Ed25519-signed credential with 6 fields (name, DOB, address, license#, class, expiry). Prove you're over 18 → you hand over all six and the signature. Show the verifier's view: everything. This is what a signed JWT/mDL does today, and it's the problem.
    * SELECTIVE DISCLOSURE — the headline mechanism. Same credential, BBS+ signed. The learner checks which fields to reveal:
        Issuer signs:  [name, DOB, address, license#, class, expiry]  → one signature
        Holder reveals: [class]                    hides: everything else
        Verifier gets:  a proof that a valid issuer signature exists
                        over messages including the revealed ones,
                        WITHOUT the signature and WITHOUT the hidden messages
      Show the verifier's actual view — the hidden fields aren't redacted, they're *absent*, and the signature itself never appears. Real verification returns true. That gap between "verified" and "saw nothing" is the lab.
    * UNLINKABILITY — the second headline. Present the same credential three times. Show the three presentations byte-by-byte: completely different, no common value, nothing to correlate. Then toggle to the Ed25519 baseline and show the same signature appearing three times — a perfect tracking cookie. The learner does the diff.
    * Predicate proof: prove DOB implies age ≥ 18 without revealing DOB. Real range proof (link bulletproofs — reuse or link, don't rebuild). The verifier learns one bit.
    * Revocation panel — the honest tension: revocation needs the verifier to check *something* about this credential, and anything checkable is potentially correlatable. Show a status-list approach and name the privacy cost. State the tradeoff; don't pretend it's solved.
    * Comparison table: BBS+ vs blind signatures (link blind-sign — the signer is blind, but the credential is still all-or-nothing at showing) vs ring signatures (link ring-sign — anonymity among a set, not selective disclosure) vs plain JWT/mDL. What each hides, from whom.
